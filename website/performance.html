§ 03 — METRIC DEFINITIONS

What We Track and Why

METRIC RATIONALE
METRIC
PURPOSE
TARGET
Win Rate
Baseline signal quality. Combined with avg win/loss gives expectancy. Kelly Criterion depends on historical win rate for sizing future trades.
> 52%
Sharpe Ratio
Risk-adjusted return. Excess return per unit of volatility. Critical for evaluating strategies that win infrequently but with high magnitude.
> 1.0
Max Drawdown
Worst peak-to-trough decline. Strategy circuit breakers trip at −5% rolling; this tracks the full account-level worst case for risk sizing decisions.
< 15%
Profit Factor
Gross wins / gross losses. A value above 1.5 indicates the strategy generates more profit than loss in aggregate dollar terms, independent of trade count.
> 1.5
IC (Information Coeff.)
Correlation of predicted signal direction with realized return. The Discovery Engine uses IC as its primary fitness function for genetic programming.
> 0.05
Avg Hold Period
Mean bars held per closed trade. Expected holding period informed by Ornstein-Uhlenbeck half-life; longer holds reduce transaction cost friction.
3–15 days
Kelly Fraction
Live half-Kelly sizing ratio from signal_outcomes. Approaches optimal sizing asymptotically with trade count. Floored at 2% until 20-trade minimum is met.
2–10%
Gate Pass Rate
Fraction of signals surviving all 15 gates. High rejection rates indicate regime detection is working; very low rates may indicate over-constraint.
5–20%
